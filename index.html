Activities of the lab will start in January, 2015, at the Université de Montréal. Tim's homepage has more informations about the research topics that will be adressed, although they can be summarized as: developing methods to measure, understand, and predict the variation of community structure in general, and species interactions in particular.

The activities of the lab will be mostly theoretical, but in close association with people working on empirical systems and questions. Candidates from all backgrounds are welcome to apply. Knowledge of C/C++, R, python or julia is a good point, but not necessary if you have a strong desire to learn.

Not all proposals listed below have secure funding yet. There are also grants and scholarships candidates can apply to, and I will provide guidance in the process.

Prospective candidates are welcome to contact Tim (t.poisot@gmail.com).

Spatial variation of species interactions (M.Sc.)

The purpose of this project is to develop (and use on empirical data) new methods to quantify the turnover of species interactions in space, especially over environmental gradients. In particular, we will focus on how the turnover of species interactions is conditionned by the turnover of species, and develop null models to correct for this effect.

Prédiction du changement de structure des réseaux (Ph.D.)

Prediction of network structure change (Ph.D.)

This is a theoretical project with a strong experimental component. Using the microbial community from the leaves of the purple pitcher plant, we will examine how environment-driven variation in species abundances and functional traits distribution shape the structure of food webs. Empirical results will be confronted to statistical and dynamical modelling. Experiments will be done in Ben Baiser's lab at the University of Florida.

Local adaptation and species interactions (M.Sc.)

Using statistical models and experiments on a bacteria-bacteriophage system, this project will focus on the two following questions: how the local eenvironment shapes the structure of interaction networkss, and how local adaptation to the environment and to other species predicts the re-organisation of the network when the environmental conditions change. Experiments will be done in Dominique Gravel's lab at the Université du Québec à Rimouski.
